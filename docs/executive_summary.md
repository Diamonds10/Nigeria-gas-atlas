# Executive summary

Nigeria Gas Atlas is a multi-layer geospatial repository that brings together public evidence on Nigeria's gas resource base, supporting infrastructure, environmental footprint, industrial demand centers, connectivity, and security exposure.

## What the project shows

The atlas is designed to answer a practical question: where do Nigeria's gas assets, industrial demand centers, logistics routes, and risk factors sit relative to one another?

A concrete recent addition is a public-source mini-grid inventory with 66 site-level records across 26 states and the FCT. That layer provides a useful starting point for understanding distributed renewable access geography, even though it is still narrower than a full solar-home-system database and should be interpreted as a public screening layer rather than a complete operating registry.

At a high level, it supports three kinds of public use:

- screening and opportunity mapping for investors
- reproducible applied research for academics and students
- operational and planning support for practitioners

## Why it is useful

The key value is not a single map or single dataset. It is the ability to compare multiple layers of evidence within one coherent analytical structure.

This makes the repository useful for:

- identifying infrastructure corridors
- locating industrial demand centers
- understanding environmental and security context around assets
- comparing upstream supply and downstream demand geography

## Public-use positioning

For external readers, the repository should be treated as a transparent evidence platform rather than a definitive commercial forecast.

It is strongest when used to:

- frame hypotheses
- support market reconnaissance
- guide field verification
- enable reproducible research

It is less appropriate for:

- final investment decisions without local validation
- legal or commercial claims relying on unverified site precision
- interpretation that ignores source caveats and date stamps
